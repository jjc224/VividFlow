About the Project


VividFlow is a web application allowing researchers to work from any platform in a standard environment. Google Chrome and Mozilla Firefox are the primary target browsers. Behind the scenes, VividFlow runs on Debian and Ubuntu systems. The back end is written in Python, using Flask and Gunicorn. The application is served through nginx and the backing database is MySQL.

Focus on the problem, not the boilerplate


Traditionally when prototyping a new algorithm, you need to write code to open the images, preprocess the image and then finally work on the problem you are trying to solve. With VividFlow you can just snap these steps together, without having to rewrite the boilerplate code and existing processing steps each time. Modules are written in C++ with the help of our VividFlow library that takes care of interfacing with the system. You can then link the data inputs from other modules into your new module. This allows you to focus on just the immediate project.

When you update a module to perform better at its task, all your algorithms will benefit from the improvement, reducing the effort of maintaining multiple projects.

Applications


VividFlow is primarily focused on computer vision research, and as a result has extensive support for video and image formats.

Potential areas of research include:

  • Object Recognition

  • Pattern Analysis

  • Object tracking

  • Feature identification

  • Object Modelling

There are no restrictions preventing VividFlow being used for other areas of research and development outside of computer vision.

Project Background


Our client expressed to our team the difficulties involved in prototyping computer vision algorithms. A previous system was demonstrated that helped alleviate similar problems for image processing. A similar system with a focus on computer vision was requested by our client. By reviewing the problems and the demonstrated system our team was able to design a system that works well for computer vision research. By allowing modules to receive multiple inputs and output multiple pieces of data the system allows for complex algorithms to be quickly connected up.

Developed for the University of Wollongong


Dr Igor Kharitonenko from the School of Computing & Information Technology requested the development of VividFlow. Over the Autumn and Spring session of 2016 our team has worked hard to provide an effective solution.
